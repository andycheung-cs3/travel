Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower, located in Paris, France, is one of the most recognizable landmarks in the world. Constructed between 1887 and 1889, it was designed by engineer Gustave Eiffel as the centerpiece for the 1889 Exposition Universelle, a world’s fair celebrating the 100th anniversary of the French Revolution. Standing at 330 meters tall today, thanks to modern antenna additions, it was initially criticized by many artists and intellectuals but has since become a beloved symbol of French ingenuity and culture.

Made of wrought iron, the tower was a groundbreaking feat of engineering at the time of its construction. It consists of over 18,000 metallic parts held together by 2.5 million rivets, and its open-lattice design was revolutionary in demonstrating how iron could be used to create both strength and elegance. Originally intended to be dismantled after 20 years, the Eiffel Tower was saved due to its usefulness as a radio transmission tower, ensuring its place as a permanent fixture in Paris.

Today, the Eiffel Tower attracts millions of visitors annually, offering breathtaking views of Paris from its observation decks. It is illuminated nightly, with sparkling lights that make it a magical sight against the Parisian skyline. Beyond tourism, it has also served as a cultural icon, appearing in countless films, artworks, and literature. The tower represents not only the spirit of innovation but also the enduring charm of Paris as the “City of Light.”
