USA history

The history of the United States is often traced back to the arrival of European settlers in the 17th century, particularly the English colonies along the Atlantic coast. These colonies grew rapidly, fueled by trade, agriculture, and migration, but tensions with Britain over taxation and governance eventually led to the American Revolution. In 1776, the Declaration of Independence was signed, marking the birth of a new nation built on ideals of liberty and self-rule.

During the 19th century, the United States expanded westward through the Louisiana Purchase, territorial acquisitions, and the concept of Manifest Destiny. This period brought both opportunity and conflict, including wars with Native American tribes, the Mexican-American War, and deep divisions over slavery. The Civil War (1861–1865) became the defining struggle of the century, ultimately preserving the Union and leading to the abolition of slavery, though racial inequality persisted long after.

The 20th century saw the United States emerge as a global power. Industrialization, immigration, and social change transformed the nation, while participation in World War I and World War II cemented its role on the world stage. The Cold War era brought both technological innovation and geopolitical rivalry, while movements for civil rights reshaped American society. By the century’s end, the U.S. stood as a leading economic and military force, continuing to influence global politics and culture into the 21st century.
